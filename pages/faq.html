FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is normal radiation?

In the United States, background radiation typically ranges from 60 to 120 nSv/h depending on elevation and local geology. Higher altitude cities like Denver naturally read higher than coastal cities like Miami. This is completely normal.

Should I be alarmed by elevated readings?

Not necessarily. Elevated readings on this dashboard mean a station is reading above the average US range — but many factors cause normal variation. Rain, seasonal changes, and detector maintenance can all affect readings. Always verify with official EPA and local emergency sources before taking action.

Is this data real-time?

Near real-time. The EPA updates station data approximately hourly. Our dashboard refreshes every 60 minutes. Some stations may show estimated data if the live fetch fails — these are clearly labeled.

Why does my city show "No Data"?

The station may be temporarily offline, undergoing maintenance, or experiencing a communication issue. EPA's network has over 140 stations — occasional gaps are normal.

Can I install this as an app?

Yes. This is a Progressive Web App (PWA). On Android, tap the browser menu and select "Add to Home Screen." On iPhone, tap the Share button and select "Add to Home Screen."

Does this work offline?

The app shell loads offline after first visit. Live radiation data requires an internet connection to fetch from EPA servers.

Is this an official government site?

No. This is an independent platform that visualizes publicly available EPA data. For official information, visit epa.gov/radnet.

How do I report a problem?

Visit our Support page or use the Contact form.
